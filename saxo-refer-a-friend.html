The brief

A referral programme that existed — but nobody used.

Saxo Bank had a referral programme in place, but it was generating almost no engagement. The technical infrastructure was there. The incentive structure was there. But users simply weren't finding it, understanding it, or completing the journey.

The goal was to revitalise the programme — make it discoverable, make the reward mechanics understandable, and design a flow that actually motivated users to refer their network and follow through to a funded account.

Layered on top: strict legal and regulatory requirements across markets. Denmark's Pyramid Scheme Act limited how referral rewards could be structured. GDPR compliance shaped what data could be used. Any solution had to work across Saxo's different market contexts without creating compliance risk.

Research

Seven usability tests and a funnel that told the real story.

Discovery ran across two parallel tracks — quantitative funnel analysis and qualitative usability testing — to understand both where users were dropping off and why.

01 — Funnel analysis

The drop-off happened after link generation

Working with data analysts and PMs, I mapped the referral funnel end to end. The clearest signal: users were generating referral links, but referred users weren't completing sign-up or funding their accounts. Drop-off happened at two points — referred users abandoning the landing page, and referrers losing track of progress.

What the data showed

The problem wasn't awareness of the reward — it was trust and clarity. Referred users were arriving on landing pages that felt inconsistent with the main platform, and referrers had no way to track whether their referral had done anything.

02 — Usability testing

7 speak-aloud sessions — both referrers and referred users

I ran 7 speak-aloud usability tests covering both sides of the referral loop. Testing old and new concepts in parallel helped isolate what was genuinely broken versus what could be improved.

  • Confusion about how "commission credits" are earned, where they appear, and how to spend them
  • Entry points in the profile menu and portfolio banner were largely ignored
  • Landing pages for referred users felt inconsistent and untrustworthy
  • A large terms & conditions CTA dominated the visual hierarchy
  • No feedback or progress tracking — referrers had no idea if their contact had signed up
The key insight from testing

Users weren't confused about what a referral programme was. They were confused about what would happen next — and that uncertainty was enough to stop them from acting.

Key insights

Three things that shaped everything.

  • Visibility drives engagement. Users weren't finding the referral feature. Entry points needed to be prominent, contextual, and hard to miss.
  • Clarity and feedback matter as much as the reward. Users didn't understand the commission credit mechanic. Until that was resolved, the incentive wasn't motivating — it was confusing.
  • Behavioural reinforcement keeps the loop alive. A referral isn't complete when someone clicks a link. The design needed to keep both sides engaged throughout the gap to a funded account.
The solution

A complete redesign of the referral journey — from entry point to funded account.

  • Dedicated referral page — replaced the cramped dialog with a full page that had space for clear instructions, reward explanation, and sharing options
  • Commission credits hub — explains what credits are, how they're earned, where they appear, and how to use them
  • Persistent funding reminder — shown until the referred user funds their account or the 90-day window closes
  • Improved landing page — redesigned for trust and consistency with the main platform
  • Friction reduction throughout — QR code sharing, simplified copy, redesigned trigger emails, empty states that guide next actions
  • Leaderboard & status dashboard (designed, paused) — fully designed and validated internally, paused pending compliance sign-off
Results

Referred users tripled. Support dropped by half.

Within one year of launch, the redesigned referral programme delivered measurable impact across the full funnel. The 50% drop in referral-related support questions was a particularly strong signal — users were no longer confused about how the programme worked.

Reflection

What I'd push for next time.

The leaderboard and referral status dashboard were the features I was most confident in — both tested well internally and addressed a real gap in the experience. Having them paused by compliance was frustrating but understandable. Next time I'd build compliance alignment into the discovery phase rather than treating it as a final gate.

I'd also introduce A/B testing earlier — particularly around reward framing and copy. The commission credits explanation worked, but there's meaningful optimisation still on the table around how the incentive is positioned for different user segments.

Taking full ownership of both UX and UI mid-project was demanding, but it meant every decision was coherent. The tradeoff was speed — more hands would have allowed faster iteration.
