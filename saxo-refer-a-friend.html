The brief

A referral programme that existed — but nobody used.

Saxo Bank had a referral programme in place, but it was generating almost no engagement. The technical infrastructure was there. The incentive structure was there. But users simply weren't finding it, understanding it, or completing the journey.

Layered on top: strict legal and regulatory requirements across markets. Denmark's Pyramid Scheme Act limited how referral rewards could be structured. GDPR compliance shaped what data could be used. Any solution had to work across Saxo's different market contexts without creating compliance risk.

The problem

The feature existed. Users just weren't engaging with it.

The referral programme had been live for some time, but the funnel data told a clear story: users were generating referral links, but referred users weren't completing sign-up or funding their accounts. Drop-off happened at two critical points — referred users abandoning the landing page, and referrers losing track of whether their referral had done anything.

  • Entry points buried in the profile menu and portfolio banner — largely ignored
  • Reward mechanics (commission credits) were confusing — users didn't know what they'd earn, where it appeared, or how to use it
  • Landing pages for referred users felt inconsistent and untrustworthy compared to the main platform
  • No feedback loop — referrers had no way to track whether their contact had signed up or funded
  • Terms & conditions CTA dominated the visual hierarchy, overshadowing the actual action
The core insight

Users weren't confused about what a referral programme was. They were confused about what would happen next — and that uncertainty was enough to stop them from acting.

Research

Two tracks running in parallel — funnel data and usability testing.

Discovery ran across two streams simultaneously: quantitative funnel analysis to understand where users were dropping off, and qualitative usability testing to understand why.

01 Funnel analysis — mapping the drop-off end to end

Working with data analysts and PMs, I mapped the referral funnel from entry point to funded account. The clearest signal: users were generating referral links, but conversion to sign-up and funding was extremely low. The biggest drop-off points were the referred user's first visit to the landing page, and the gap between sign-up and funding.

What the data showed

The problem wasn't awareness of the reward — it was trust and clarity. Referred users were arriving on pages that felt disconnected from the main platform, and referrers had no visibility into what happened after they shared their link.

02 Usability testing — 7 sessions across both sides of the referral loop

I ran 7 speak-aloud usability tests covering both the referrer experience and the referred user experience. Testing old and new concepts in parallel helped isolate what was genuinely broken versus what could be improved with clearer copy and hierarchy.

  • Commission credits were not understood — users didn't know what they were, where they'd appear, or how to spend them
  • The terms & conditions link was visually dominant, creating anxiety rather than reassurance
  • No confirmation or progress state after sharing — referrers felt like they'd sent a link into a void
  • Landing page looked like a generic marketing page, not a trusted financial platform
Key insight from testing

Every point of confusion mapped back to the same root cause: users couldn't picture what would happen next. The design needed to make the entire journey — for both sides — feel predictable and trustworthy.

Research summary

Three things needed to change: visibility of the feature, clarity of the reward mechanics, and a feedback loop that kept both sides engaged until the referral was complete. Everything else was downstream of these.

Process

Three weeks. One sprint. Discover, define, deliver.

Week 1 — Discover
Validating the scope before designing anything

Stakeholder interviews, UX audit across Investor and TraderGO, competitive analysis, and desk research on self-help behaviour in financial products. The goal was to surface the real problem — not just execute the brief as given.

UX audit — Saxo Investor desktop help page vs TraderGO modal

Week 2 — Define & develop
Requirements first, wireframes second

I ran a mini workshop with the team to translate research findings into design requirements: preserve task context, reduce access friction, stay within the design system, maintain support optionality, and enable future scalability. Low-fidelity wireframes followed, with in-house testing for early alignment.

Early wireframe explorations — testing different interaction models

Week 3 — Deliver
Guerrilla testing in the wild, then handoff

Built an interactive prototype and tested with 8 participants in a guerrilla format. Validated the tab separation, chat-first presentation, and state persistence behaviour. Then went straight to high-fidelity across all three platforms and handed off with full specs, annotations, and design system documentation.

Interactive prototype — flow used in guerrilla testing

The solution

A complete redesign of the referral journey — from entry point to funded account.

The solution addressed every friction point identified in research, working within the design system and legal constraints. Several features were fully designed and validated — one was paused before shipping due to compliance concerns in certain markets.

  • Dedicated referral page — replaced the cramped dialog with a full page, giving space for clear instructions, reward explanation, and sharing options
  • Commission credits hub — a dedicated section explaining what credits are, how they're earned, where they appear, and how to use them
  • Persistent funding reminder — shown to the referrer until the referred user funds their account or the 90-day window closes
  • Redesigned landing page — rebuilt for trust and consistency with the main platform, clearer hierarchy, better instructions
  • Friction reduction throughout — QR code sharing, simplified copy, redesigned trigger emails, empty states that guide next actions
  • Leaderboard & status dashboard (designed, paused) — fully designed and validated internally, paused pending compliance sign-off in regulated markets

Final hi-fi designs — all states across Investor, TraderGO and mobile

Results

Referred users tripled. Support dropped by half.

Within one year of launch, the redesigned referral programme delivered measurable impact across the full funnel. The 50% drop in referral-related support questions was a particularly strong signal — users were no longer confused about how the programme worked.

Reflection

What I'd push for next time.

The leaderboard and referral status dashboard were the features I was most confident in — both tested well internally and addressed a real gap in the experience. Having them paused by compliance was frustrating but understandable. Next time I'd build compliance alignment into the discovery phase rather than treating it as a final gate.

Taking full ownership of both UX and UI mid-project was demanding, but it meant every decision stayed coherent. The tradeoff was speed — something to factor in earlier when scoping future projects.

I'd also introduce A/B testing earlier — particularly around reward framing and copy. The commission credits explanation worked, but there's meaningful optimisation still on the table around how the incentive is positioned for different user segments.
